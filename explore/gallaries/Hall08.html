Early Life

Tutankhamun, often called King Tut, was an ancient Egyptian pharaoh of the 18th Dynasty. He became king at a very young age — likely around 8 or 9 years old — during the turbulent aftermath of his father Akhenaten's religious revolution.

1332 – 1323 BC

His Reign

His reign was relatively short and not politically significant compared to other pharaohs. However, he is known for restoring traditional Egyptian religion after the controversial reign of his predecessor, Akhenaten, who had introduced the worship of a single god.

c. 1323 BC

His Death

Tutankhamun died at about 18 or 19 years old, and the exact cause of his death remains uncertain. Some studies suggest illness or injury, while others point to genetic conditions inherited from the close bloodlines of the royal family.

1922

The Discovery

What made Tutankhamun world-famous was the discovery of his nearly intact tomb by British archaeologist Howard Carter in the Valley of the Kings. The tomb, known as KV62, contained over 5,000 artifacts, including his iconic gold funerary mask.

Today, Tutankhamun is one of the most famous figures of ancient Egypt — a symbol of the richness, mystery, and artistry of the pharaonic civilization.
