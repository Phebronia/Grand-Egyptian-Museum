The Grand Egyptian Museum is a landmark destination dedicated to preserving, presenting, and celebrating the remarkable heritage of ancient Egyptian civilization.

OUR STORY

A NEW HOME FOR EGYPT’S TIMELESS LEGACY

Created to share the heritage of ancient Egypt through a world-class cultural experience, the Grand Egyptian Museum invites visitors to encounter thousands of years of history, artistry, belief, and innovation in one extraordinary destination.

The GEM complex covers about 500,000 square meters, making it one of the largest museum complexes in the world. Its main building includes a 92,000-square-meter museum section and a 40,000-square-meter commercial section.

ARCHITECTURE

INSPIRED BY THE PYRAMIDS, DESIGNED FOR THE FUTURE

The design reflects a dialogue between heritage and innovation. Chosen through an international competition of 1,557 firms from 83 countries, the concept presents the museum inspired by rays from the three pyramids.

Blending modern design with references to ancient Egyptian architecture, the building frames views of the pyramids while incorporating local materials, natural ventilation strategies, and environmentally conscious systems. This union of symbolism, efficiency, and place gives the museum its powerful identity as a contemporary cultural landmark.

SUSTAINABILITY

A MUSEUM WITH A RESPONSIBLE VISION

The Grand Egyptian Museum is committed to being a global leader in sustainability by integrating scientific practices into its design and operations. Its efforts are guided by three main goals: environmental sustainability, social sustainability, and financial sustainability.


The museum has also been recognized for its green building approach and sustainability standards, including EDGE ADVANCE certification, making it the first green museum in Africa and the Middle East.
